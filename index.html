Call for Papers

Quantum information science is rapidly evolving from isolated experimental platforms toward networked quantum systems that operate across multiple quantum computers and support a wide range of quantum applications. Recent advances in large-scale or even global-scale entanglement distribution with the help of fibers, repeaters and satellites indicate that quantum capabilities are increasingly realized in distributed and networked manners, moving beyond end-to-end transmission toward wider-area infrastructures.

In such environments, quantum computers are no longer confined to single devices but instead operate across the world, connected through entanglements. Yet building networked quantum systems and applications remains a formidable challenge. Fundamental physical limitations and resource constraints make long-distance entanglement distribution extremely costly and fragile, while the unique properties of quantum information prohibit straightforward use of classical error correction or amplification. These challenges highlight the urgent need for new abstractions, models, and algorithms that can bridge physical constraints with system design.

The 1st International Workshop on Networked Quantum Systems and Applications (NetQSA 2026) aims to bring together researchers from networking, distributed systems, and quantum information science to explore these challenges. The workshop will focus on the design, analysis, and implementation of networked quantum systems and their applications.

Topics of Interest

The workshop solicits original research contributions on networked quantum systems and their applications. Both theoretical and experimental work are welcome. We invite submissions on a wide range of topics, including but not limited to:

  • Networked quantum systems and architectures
  • Protocol design for networked quantum systems
  • Theoretical foundation for networked quantum systems
  • Integration of classical and quantum networks and systems
  • Distributed quantum computing
  • Distributed/entangled quantum sensing
  • Distributed quantum algorithms and protocols
  • Performance evaluation of quantum systems over networks
  • Resource management in networked quantum systems
  • Scalability, reliability, and flexibility in networked quantum systems
  • Measurements and network tomography
  • Hardware design for quantum repeaters and transduction
  • Transduction and interoperability between heterogeneous quantum systems
  • Quantum-secure technologies and QKD
  • Classical networking for quantum network design
  • Topology design for quantum networks
  • Non-terrestrial and satellite quantum networks
  • Purification, error mitigation and error correction
  • Quantum machine learning
  • Quantum data centers and edge computing
  • AI/ML (including QML) for networked quantum systems
  • Quantum for classical networks and applications
  • Experimental platforms, testbeds, and prototypes

Submission Guidelines

We invite two types of submissions:

  • Full Papers: Up to 6 pages (including figures and references), formatted according to the IEEE conference template. Submissions must present original, unpublished work that is not under concurrent review elsewhere.
  • Short Papers / Extended Abstracts: Up to 2 pages (excluding references), following the same format. This track is intended for early-stage work, position papers, and work-in-progress.

All submissions will undergo a peer-review process and will be evaluated based on relevance, technical quality, and potential to stimulate discussion at the workshop. At least one author of each accepted paper is required to attend the workshop and present the work. Accepted papers will be included in the ICNP 2026 workshop proceedings and published in IEEE Xplore (subject to IEEE approval).
